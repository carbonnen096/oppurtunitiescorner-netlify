How to Secure a Seasonal Fruit Picking Job in Europe with Visa Sponsorship

Seasonal fruit picking jobs offer a unique opportunity to earn money while traveling, experiencing new cultures, and working outdoors in beautiful landscapes. If you're looking for agricultural work in countries like Poland, the United Kingdom, or even New Zealand, this guide will walk you through the entire process—from finding legitimate employers who offer visa sponsorship to preparing for your working adventure abroad.

Pro Tip: Start your search 3-6 months before the season begins. The best opportunities fill up quickly, especially those offering visa sponsorship and accommodation.

1. What Is Seasonal Fruit Picking Work?

Seasonal agricultural work involves helping farmers harvest their crops during peak seasons. Depending on the region and time of year, you might be picking:

  • Summer fruits like strawberries, cherries, and blueberries
  • Autumn harvests including apples, pears, and grapes
  • Citrus fruits in warmer climates during winter months
  • Various vegetables and other crops

Typical Responsibilities:

  • Hand-picking fruits at peak ripeness
  • Sorting and grading produce by quality
  • Packing fruits for distribution
  • Basic farm maintenance and equipment operation

2. Why Choose Visa Sponsorship Opportunities?

Working abroad legally requires proper documentation. Visa sponsorship from employers offers several advantages:

  • Legal Protection: Work without worrying about immigration issues
  • Guaranteed Employment: Your job is secured before you arrive
  • Additional Benefits: Many sponsored positions include housing or transportation
  • Future Opportunities: Successful workers often get invited back for future seasons

3. Top Countries for Fruit Picking Jobs with Visa Sponsorship

🇵🇱 Poland: Seasonal Agricultural Work Visa

  • Season: May to October (varies by crop)
  • Visa Type: Seasonal Work Visa (up to 9 months)
  • Requirements: Job offer from registered Polish employer, clean criminal record
  • Average Pay: 15-25 PLN/hour (≈ $4-6 USD) + often includes accommodation

How to Apply: Through EURES job portal or agencies like OTTO Work Force

🇬🇧 United Kingdom: Seasonal Worker Visa

  • Season: April to November (peak June-September)
  • Visa Type: Tier 5 Seasonal Worker Visa (up to 6 months)
  • Requirements: Certificate of Sponsorship, £1,270 in savings
  • Average Pay: £9.50-10.50/hour + often piece-rate bonuses

Approved Recruiters: Concordia, AG Recruitment, Pro-Force

🇳🇿 New Zealand: Recognised Seasonal Employer Scheme

  • Season: November to April (Southern Hemisphere summer)
  • Program: RSE Scheme (up to 7 months in 11-month period)
  • Requirements: Medical clearance, basic English skills
  • Average Pay: NZD $18-22/hour + accommodation assistance

Top Employers: Mr Apple NZ, T&G Global, Seeka Ltd

4. Your Step-by-Step Application Guide

Research & Planning
Identify target countries and seasons. Consider language requirements, climate, and typical working conditions.
Document Preparation
Get your passport ready, prepare a simple CV highlighting any physical work experience, and obtain necessary clearances.
Job Search
Use official channels like government portals or approved recruiters. Avoid middlemen asking for large fees.
Visa Application
Once you have a job offer, apply for the appropriate visa with your employer's sponsorship documents.
Travel Preparation
Arrange insurance, vaccinations if needed, and plan your arrival to accommodate any quarantine requirements.

5. Maximizing Your Success

  • Apply Early: The best positions fill months in advance
  • Highlight Relevant Skills: Even non-farm experience like construction or landscaping helps
  • Be Flexible: Willingness to work in remote locations increases opportunities
  • Learn Basic Local Phrases: Shows commitment and helps daily life

6. Life as a Seasonal Worker

💼 Work Conditions

Expect early starts (often 5-6am), 8-10 hour days, and weekend work during peak season. Payment is typically hourly or piece-rate (pay per quantity picked).

🏡 Living Arrangements

Most employers provide basic shared housing (often dorm-style). Costs are usually deducted from wages at reasonable rates.

🌍 Cultural Experience

You'll work alongside people from around the world. Take time to explore local areas on days off and immerse yourself in the culture.

7. Is This Right For You?

Seasonal fruit picking offers adventure and income, but requires:

  • Good physical stamina (standing, bending, lifting all day)
  • Adaptability to changing weather conditions
  • Comfort with communal living arrangements
  • Positive attitude toward repetitive tasks
